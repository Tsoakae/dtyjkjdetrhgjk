Student Representative Council

Student Representative Council is a representative structure through which students in tertiary schools can become involved in the affairs of the school, working in partnership with the school management and staff for the benefit of the school and its students.

Why the SRC ?

  • The role of the SRC is to promote an environment conducive to educational and personal development.
  • To support management and Staff in the development of the school
  • To represent the views of the students on matters of general concern to them
  • To enhance communication between students and Management

SRC Positions

PositionRole
President
  • Preside at all SRC meetings.
  • Advice APA leaders on Students' welfare.
  • Cooperate with the Management for stability.
Vice President
  • Liason with other bodies on behalf of SRC.
  • Serve on student award Juries.
  • Attend SRC Executive meetings.
Treasurer
  • Provide guidance and support to the SU.
  • Plan student activities, programs and sessions.
  • Promote APA and students' membership and encourage registration.
